Recap from findings about Study.com

  • - Study.com's mission is, “[They] believe everyone deserves access to affordable, high-quality education.”

  • - Their focus is on primarily high-school students and adults, even though they catered to younger students as well. They provide digital courses that are college accredited at a reduced cost, which helps students who need to transfer to college, but are low-income and cannot pay the high cost for accrediation elsewhere.

  • - Study.com was cofounded in 2002, by Adrián Ridner, and Ben Wilson. The curriculum featured on their website and mobile app is coordinated by Jessica Bayliss, the director of Education. While they have other teachers for various academic subjects, I concentrated on their math teachers: Luke Winspur, M.A., Erin Lennon, Ph.D., and Kathryn Maloney, M.S.

Positive Impact Limitations
What specifically are the most helpful aspects of this case study?
  • - Goal is aligned with what I am trying to achieve.
  • - This method they implement has proven to be successful with palpable results nationwide.
  • - The credits given are accredited at a collegiate level, which I can find out how to make the courses I imported from a credible insitution transferable to college like UCs, CSUs, and Privates.
  1. Study.com gives new students a pathway to achieve a degree at a U.S institution.
  2. As previous mentioned, they help students from K-12, and adults who need additional help outside of the classroom, or college credits
  3. They helped over 30 million students monthly that use their study tools and educational videos to achieve their academic goals.
Issues with Study.com
  • Students may have issues accessing websites or mobile apps due to lack of internet access & or low-quality devices.
    • The learning may not be effective prepation; thus, it might not translate once students are
    doing college-level work independently.
  • The user interface on the website or mobile may be unappealing to students.
  • Independent study may be unwelcoming for students who are use to a traditional classroom
    setting.
  • Content hosted could be unengaging to some students because their platform lacks a personal connection without meaningful connections with peers
How might technology be used differently or better to improve the
effectiveness of the app?

It can be better by changing the UI to be more welcoming and engaging to users on their
platform. Furthermore, they can also feature games or project-making activities that bring
people together to create a sense of community, so they are eagered to continue learning
online. There can be platforms that connect with them, which provide safe areas for people
to learn with sufficient internet.
