ROOT Cause #1

Sports injuries in the NCAA causes a decline in academic
performance not only due to the lack of prevenetative measures in NCAA but also due to the absence of accomadation to
student-athlete's injuries.
ROOT Cause #2

Student-athletes underperform academically because school
insitutions worldwide fail to incorporate time to educate
student-athletes about sport-specific interventions besides
sports which stunts their academic potential they can achieve.

Sources

Evidence #1 (Root Cause #1)

  1. The NCAA Injury Surveillance Program (NCAA-ISP) did an analysis over 5 academic years; 2009-2014, where NCAA estimated cumulative average of injuries nationwide. During this period, 1,053,370 injuries were estimated to have occurred during an estimated 176.7 million athlete- exposures to potential injury (i.e., one athlete’s participation in one competition or one practice).

  2. Among all 25 sports, an estimated 28,860,299 practice athlete-exposures and 6,472,952 competition athlete-exposures occurred each year...overall, 21.9% of all injuries required ≥7 days before return to full participation (competition: 24.6%; practice: 20.5%) (Figure 1).

Evidence #2 (Root Cause #2)

  1. TTS, or Topsport Talent Schools, is founded in Netherlands in 1991. This research studied the effect of attending a TTS than a mainstream schools. From their particpatiants of former talented athletes from six sport federations they concluded a couple of points.
    a. The lack of increased sport performance levels of talented athletes who attended TTS could be explained by the fact that there are rarely sport specific training facilities available at TTS (Flatau and Emrich, 2011).
    b. Based on the theory that less time spent at school leads to less school bonding and lower school motivation, talented athletes who attended TTS are expected to have achieved a lower school performance level compared to those at mainstream secondary schools.
Explanation #1

Approximately 1 in 5 injuries are serious injuries that requires active medical attention which means student-athletes cannot return to academics; thus, a decline in academic performance. However, this figure is an underestimate because athletic trainers are not present at every practice, who can provide prevention tatics. NCAA is not following its due diligence of keeping the turnover rate low of athletic trainers, hence, the shortage of athletic trainers causes every individual to get less focused treatment for an athlete to recover.

Explanation #2

Although student-athletes understand how to prevent a chronic decline
in their sports performance, they are unaware on how to maintain
academic displcine due to lack of emotional or physiological support
being provided in TTS schools. Consequently, instead of being
multifaceted at a collegiate level, TTS schools undermine their potential because they don't provide facilities to support them academically,
instead they are more keen on sports achievements.

Implication for both root causes

Overall, the uptick of not only sports injuries, but also mental injuries stems from NCAA not directing enough funding to TTS schools in order to prevent academic decline. That decline continues as a cycle of a student-athlete facing an impactful which results in emotional trauma recovering from that injury, but instead of being given resources and support to recover mentally, they are stuck in isolation trying to overcame that academic slump. Ultimately, student-athletes can perform to their potential through additional support outside of the classroom and a curriculum tailored to support them emotionally.
